First sample
Gunna was very smitten by Jón when young, she would be on the swings looking at Jón be around the others and look at her flower. Jón never talked to her, just grasping her flower tighter.

She let the flower decide whether she should talk to him. She started by numbering the petals from $1$ to $N$ counter-clockwise, where $N$ is the number of petals.

She starts at petal $1$ and says “He loves me”. She leaves this petal be and moves onto petal $2$. She then says “He loves me not” and rips petal $2$ off. She then moves to the third petal and says “He loves me”. She continues like this, alternating between the two, going around and around until there is only one petal left.

Can you count along with Gunna and tell her what the number of the last petal will be?

Input

The integer $1 \leq N$, the number of petals on the flower at the start.

Output

One line with the number of the petal that’s left at the end.

Exaplanation of Sample Inputs

Gunna does the following in the first sample:

  • She starts at petal $1$, and says “He loves me”.

  • She moves to petal $2$, says “He loves me not”, and rips the petal off.

  • She moves to petal $3$, and says “He loves me”.

  • She moves to petal $4$, says “He loves me not”, and rips the petal off.

  • She moves to petal $5$, and says “He loves me”.

  • She moves to petal $1$, says “He loves me not”, and rips the petal off.

  • She moves to petal $3$, which is the last petal.
